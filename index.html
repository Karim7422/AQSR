ISO 22301 serves as the global benchmark for Business Continuity Management (BCM), evolving from the foundation laid by British Standard BS 25999 and regional standards. It is strategically crafted to safeguard businesses from various forms of disruptions, encompassing extreme weather events, fires, floods, natural calamities, theft, IT failures, staff absenteeism, or even terrorist incidents.
ISO 50001 is utilized by organizations of various sizes worldwide to effectively manage and minimize energy consumption and associated costs. It provides a robust framework for implementing an Energy Management System (EnMS). This standard caters to a wide spectrum of businesses, ranging from large retailers to small-scale manufacturers and enterprises. It equips organizations with the tools to enhance their resilience against fluctuations in energy costs and availability, thereby fostering sustainability and efficiency.
The ISO 27001 standard employs a process-oriented approach to establish, implement, operate, monitor, review, maintain, and enhance an organization's information security management system.
A Food Safety Management System (FSMS) is a proactive strategy employed by organizations to detect, prevent, and mitigate food-borne hazards from entering the food chain, thus enhancing the organization's food safety performance. ISO 22000 serves as a risk-based management tool specifically designed for the food industry, aimed at ensuring food safety for human consumption.
The ISO 45001 standard offers a framework for effectively managing the prevention of work-related injuries, illnesses, and fatalities. Its primary goal is to enhance and ensure a safe and healthy work environment for both employees and any other individuals who may come into contact with the organization.
ISO 14001:2015 is an international standard for Environmental Management Systems (EMS), applicable to organizations regardless of their size or industry. This includes various sectors such as rolling mills, steel plants, hospitals, paper mills, medical devices, and healthcare in India. ISO 14001 sets forth requirements for establishing an effective EMS, aiming to help organizations manage their environmental responsibilities efficiently and systematically.
